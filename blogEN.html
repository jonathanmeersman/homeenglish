ATELIER VIEW DECEMBER 2016

TEXT 'THE UNBEARABLE LIGHTNESS OF READING'

     "The unbearable lightness of reading
    Seventh part, Karenins Smile
    Chapter three"


    Only diacritical/reading marks and spaces are being read.


    Starting from a personal experience; frequent reading, creates questions. What is, after a while, the benefit of reading so much; what do you get from it or achieve from it¿ It is often proved that reading is good for you; it has a big influence on you being. You define and show a part of yourself with books. On a personal base, this was still not a good enough answer to me. 
Every book that I read has influence on me. It was interesting to create some kind of an universal structure. Only working with the data that every book has; the indications in the text -reading marks- where you read over. Through every book is an abstract pattern show in such kind of a form, every time a variation of, formal, its own. This reminded me of creative writing. ‘Gadsby’, a book of Wright, as an example; a whole book where, in the story, the letter ‘e’ isn’t used a single time. Even other kinds of forms where all the words are crossed out in black. This as a step further, only verbally. A kind of creative writing form.

    Using this ‘method’ brings a lot of contradictions. The most contradicted is making books universal. This isn’t an accessible viewpoint for everyone; the language where the reading marks where read aloud gives a limitation. Knowing there were some people in the audience that spoke only English; where the reading marks read and spoken, aware, in the Dutch language. This factor to work with and mostly counterbalance to the universal aspect and its problems. 
Eventually gives repeatedly speaking ‘space’ for confusion, disorder with the pronunciation; like a ‘tongue-twister’. It is rather difficult and exhausting, to put up with this gets strongly tested after a while. Here was the thought to use google translate; nevertheless got also the program it very difficult with the pronunciation. So I decided to use this difficulty to show the ‘struggle’ and problematic. It is other simply the reading of an abstract formed noise; not the fact that you have to understand it, besides reading it. However is this simplicity rather difficult. Here was the possibility to play with the title of the book. ‘The unbearable lightness of being’ becomes ‘the unbearable lightness of reading’.


    With this discourse that I did for myself, I created an abstract language that was a pure form of nothing -not being-; a banality. This reminded me of the literary discours of Huge Ball in the Dada-movement. How they handled nothing what possessed everything. Casting questions, giving not a single answer but at the main time handeling everything. Their style of banalizing and humanize is a form to handle such data. 
The most important question was what I was going to present. Because it started from a personal argument, I decided to use a personal favorite, ‘The unbearable lightness of being of Milan Kundera. The used book was read before with the awareness of leaving marks behing in the book. Marks put between pages to remind me of interesting and personal aspects in the story. Just like Dada I decided to use the factor of coincidence. The first mark showed me something on the left page, at the end of a chapter; while the seventh part of the book started on the right side. The second remark was on the spread where, again, on the left page, on the end of a chapter, was a reminder for myself. On the right page was the beginning of chapter seven, Karenins Smile, of the seventh part of the book.


    This monologe, discourse, literary statement was a individual referred data. The public, for whom are a few visible in the video, was actually not an important factor. I was doing something for myself; by expressing and reading out aloud, I show something -for myself-. A kind of literary statement for creative reading.
